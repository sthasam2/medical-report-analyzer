DESCRIPTION OF PROCEDURE: The patient was taken to the operating room, and after adequate anesthesia, the patient was placed in the dorsal lithotomy position on the operating room table. The genital and perineal regions and urethra were prepped and draped in sterile fashion. A 21 French scope was manipulated easily into the patient's urethra and into the bladder without difficulty. The stent was easily visible on the dome of the bladder and was removed with a flexible grasper. No other foreign bodies or mucosal lesions were seen within the bladder. The bladder was drained and the scope was removed. The patient tolerated the procedure well.
